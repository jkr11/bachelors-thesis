Endowing the structure of tensor networks with a symmetry group
yiels a speedup over traditional techniques. Many hamiltonians
exhibit such a symmetry. This requires building a symbolic backen
making use of fusion trees ---referred to as clebsch-gordan-coefficients --- which immedeately suggest an extension
of the theory to anyonic networks and categorical networks. 

Here, we review the theory behind symmetric tensor networks and
anyonic tensor networks, and we show how both of these are governed
by a categorical formalism. We further show how to run categorical
braiding circuits.

Finally, we propose an outlook on higher structures such as module categories
which also admit generalized CG-coefficients, as well as MPO-symmetries
